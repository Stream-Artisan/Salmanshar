Salman Ali, Advocate High Court, is an accomplished legal practitioner and consultant based in Karachi, Pakistan. As an energetic and competitive law graduate, Salman Ali possesses a strong academic foundation complemented by years of hands-on experience in the legal field.

Arbitration

Arbitration is a part of Alternative dispute Resolution mechanism that benefits parties who want to avoid the normal lengthy resource to the local courts for settlement of disputes. It’s a legal technique for the resolution of dispute outside the courts, wherein the parties to a dispute refer it to one or more persons namely arbitrator by whose decision they are bound to agree.

Have A Questions?

1 What types of cases does your law firm handle?
We handle a wide range of legal matters including criminal law, civil litigation, family disputes, corporate and business law, property matters, and constitutional petitions. Our team offers both advisory and litigation services across various courts in Pakistan.
2 How can I book a consultation with your lawyer?
You can book a consultation by calling our office, filling out the contact form on our website, or visiting our office directly. We recommend booking an appointment in advance to ensure proper time is allocated to your case.
3 What documents should I bring for my first legal consultation?
For your first meeting, please bring any relevant documents such as CNIC copies, legal notices, contracts, FIRs, court orders, or other case-related material. This helps us understand your situation and provide accurate advice.
4Do you offer legal services for overseas Pakistanis?
Yes, we provide specialized legal support for overseas Pakistanis, including property disputes, power of attorney, family matters, and representation in local courts. We also facilitate remote communication through email, WhatsApp, or video calls.
5 What are your fees and payment procedures?
Our legal fees vary depending on the nature and complexity of the case. We provide a transparent fee structure during the initial consultation. Payment can be made via cash, bank transfer, or cheque, and receipts are issued for all transactions.
